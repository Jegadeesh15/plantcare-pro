## Step-by-Step User Guide

### Step 1: Plant Selection

#### Accessing Plant Selection
1. Click "Start Diagnosis" or "Diagnose" from any page
2. You'll be directed to the plant selection interface
3. Browse plants by category or use the search function

#### Plant Categories
- **Cereal Crops**: Rice, Wheat, Corn/Maize
- **Vegetable Crops**: Tomato, Potato, Soybean
- **Fruit Crops**: Apple, Citrus, Grape, Banana
- **Cash Crops**: Cotton, Sugarcane

#### Selecting Your Plant
1. **Browse by Category**: Click category buttons to filter plants
2. **Search Function**: Type plant name in the search box
3. **Plant Cards**: Each plant shows:
   - High-quality plant image
   - Common and scientific names
   - Plant category and description
   - Number of diagnosable diseases
4. **Make Selection**: Click on your plant to proceed

#### Plant Information Display
Each plant card includes:
- **Visual Identification**: Clear, high-resolution images
- **Scientific Classification**: Proper botanical names
- **Description**: Key characteristics and uses
- **Disease Count**: Number of diseases we can diagnose
- **Common Diseases**: Preview of major diseases affecting the plant

### Step 2: Symptom Checker

#### Understanding the Symptom Interface
After selecting your plant, you'll see:
- **Selected Plant Summary**: Confirmation of your plant choice
- **Symptom Categories**: Organized by plant parts
- **Selected Symptoms Tracker**: Real-time count of chosen symptoms
- **Custom Symptom Input**: Add symptoms not listed

#### Symptom Categories Explained

##### Leaf Symptoms
Common leaf-related problems:
- **Brown spots on leaves**: Circular or irregular brown markings
- **Yellow spots on leaves**: Yellowing areas or patches
- **White spots on leaves**: Light-colored or bleached areas
- **Holes in leaves**: Physical damage or eaten areas
- **Leaf curling**: Leaves rolling or curling inward/outward
- **Leaf wilting**: Drooping or limp leaves
- **Leaf yellowing**: Overall yellowing of leaf tissue
- **Leaf dropping**: Premature leaf fall
- **Spots with rings**: Concentric ring patterns (bull's eye)
- **Fuzzy growth on leaves**: Mold or fungal growth

##### Stem Symptoms
Stem and branch issues:
- **Brown lesions on stem**: Dark spots or patches on stems
- **Stem rot**: Soft, decaying stem tissue
- **Stem wilting**: Drooping or weak stems
- **Stem discoloration**: Unusual coloring of stem tissue
- **Stem cankers**: Sunken, dead areas on stems
- **Stem breakage**: Weak or broken stems

##### Fruit Symptoms
Fruit and produce problems:
- **Spots on fruit**: Discolored areas on fruit surface
- **Fruit rot**: Soft, decaying fruit tissue
- **Fruit discoloration**: Unusual fruit coloring
- **Sunken spots on fruit**: Depressed areas on fruit
- **Fruit cracking**: Splits or cracks in fruit skin
- **Premature fruit drop**: Fruit falling before maturity

##### Root Symptoms
Underground root issues:
- **Root rot**: Decaying root system
- **Root discoloration**: Unusual root coloring
- **Stunted root growth**: Poor root development
- **Root lesions**: Damaged areas on roots

##### General Symptoms
Overall plant health indicators:
- **Stunted growth**: Slower than normal plant development
- **Poor yield**: Reduced fruit or grain production
- **Plant death**: Complete plant failure
- **Reduced vigor**: Overall weak plant appearance
- **Abnormal growth**: Unusual growth patterns

#### Selecting Symptoms
1. **Review Categories**: Look through each symptom category
2. **Check Boxes**: Click checkboxes next to observed symptoms
3. **Multiple Selection**: Select all applicable symptoms
4. **Real-time Tracking**: Watch the selected symptoms counter
5. **Remove Symptoms**: Uncheck boxes to remove symptoms

#### Adding Custom Symptoms
1. **Locate Input Box**: Find "Add Custom Symptom" section
2. **Describe Symptom**: Type detailed description
3. **Click Add**: Add the custom symptom to your list
4. **Review Addition**: See custom symptom in selected list

#### Best Practices for Symptom Selection
- **Be Specific**: Choose symptoms that closely match what you observe
- **Multiple Symptoms**: Select all relevant symptoms for better accuracy
- **Timing Matters**: Consider when symptoms first appeared
- **Severity Assessment**: Note if symptoms are mild, moderate, or severe
- **Distribution**: Consider if symptoms affect entire plant or specific areas

### Step 3: AI Diagnosis

#### Diagnosis Process
1. **Submit Symptoms**: Click "Continue to Diagnosis" button
2. **AI Analysis**: System analyzes your inputs against disease database
3. **Loading Screen**: Brief processing time (usually 2-5 seconds)
4. **Results Display**: Comprehensive diagnosis results appear

#### Understanding Diagnosis Results

##### Result Layout
Each diagnosis result includes:
- **Ranking**: Numbered by likelihood (#1 most likely)
- **Disease Name**: Common name of the identified disease
- **Scientific Name**: Pathogen causing the disease
- **Confidence Score**: Percentage indicating diagnosis certainty
- **Confidence Level**: High (70%+), Medium (40-69%), Low (<40%)

##### Confidence Scoring System
- **90-95%**: Very High Confidence - Strong symptom match
- **70-89%**: High Confidence - Good symptom correlation
- **40-69%**: Medium Confidence - Partial symptom match
- **20-39%**: Low Confidence - Limited symptom correlation
- **<20%**: Very Low Confidence - Poor symptom match

##### Disease Information Display
For each potential disease:
- **Pathogen Type**: Fungal, Bacterial, Viral, or Oomycete
- **Symptom Count**: Number of known symptoms for this disease
- **Treatment Count**: Available treatment options
- **Symptom Matches**: Which of your symptoms matched
- **Match Details**: Specific symptom correlations

#### Interpreting Results

##### High Confidence Results (70%+)
- **Action**: Proceed with recommended treatments
- **Reliability**: Very likely accurate diagnosis
- **Next Steps**: Review treatment options immediately

##### Medium Confidence Results (40-69%)
- **Action**: Consider additional symptom observation
- **Reliability**: Possible but not certain diagnosis
- **Next Steps**: Monitor plant for additional symptoms

##### Low Confidence Results (<40%)
- **Action**: Seek additional expert consultation
- **Reliability**: Uncertain diagnosis
- **Next Steps**: Consider professional plant pathologist consultation

#### No Clear Diagnosis
If no confident diagnosis is found:
- **Possible Causes**: Environmental stress, nutrient deficiency, pest damage
- **Recommendations**: 
  - Add more specific symptoms
  - Consult local agricultural extension
  - Consider soil and water testing
  - Monitor plant for symptom development

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Is PlantCare Pro free to use?**
A: Yes, basic plant disease diagnosis is free. Advanced features and premium support may require subscription in the future.

**Q: How accurate is the AI diagnosis?**
A: Our AI system achieves 95% accuracy rate based on comprehensive testing. Accuracy depends on symptom selection quality and disease complexity.

**Q: Can I use PlantCare Pro on my mobile device?**
A: Yes, the platform is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets through your web browser.

**Q: Do I need to create an account?**
A: No account is required for basic diagnosis functionality. Future versions may offer account features for tracking and history.

### Technical Questions

**Q: What browsers are supported?**
A: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest versions recommended). Mobile browsers are also supported.

**Q: Why is the website loading slowly?**
A: Check your internet connection, clear browser cache, or try a different browser. Contact support if problems persist.

**Q: Can I save my diagnosis results?**
A: Currently, results are not saved automatically. We recommend taking screenshots or notes. Account-based saving is planned for future releases.

### Diagnosis Questions

**Q: What if I can't find my plant in the database?**
A: Try searching with the scientific name or select a similar plant. Contact support to request addition of new plants.

**Q: What if the diagnosis seems wrong?**
A: Consider adding more symptoms, review your symptom selections, or consult a local agricultural expert for confirmation.

**Q: Can I diagnose multiple diseases on one plant?**
A: Yes, plants can have multiple diseases. The system will show multiple possibilities ranked by confidence score.

### Treatment Questions

**Q: Are the recommended treatments safe?**
A: All treatments are based on established agricultural practices. Always read product labels and follow safety instructions.

**Q: What if recommended products aren't available locally?**
A: Look for products with similar active ingredients or consult local agricultural suppliers for alternatives.

**Q: Can I use organic treatments only?**
A: Yes, the platform provides organic and natural treatment options alongside conventional chemical treatments.
